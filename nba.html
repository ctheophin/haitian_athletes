NBA Haitian Athletes

Blake Griffin

A power forward for the Detroit Pistons.

Stats Bio Highlights

Blake Griffin is a professional basketball player for the Detroit Pistons. He was selected 1st overall in the 2009 NBA draft by the Los Angeles Clippers. He was born on March 16, 1989, in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma. His father, Tommy Griffin is of Afro-Haitian descent and his mother, Gail Griffin is white. Griffin played college basketball for the University of Oklahoma. Griffin is a 5x All Star, 4x All-NBA, and 2010-2011 Rookie of the Year. Check out his highlights!

Skal Labissière

A power forward for the Sacramento Kings.

Stats Bio Highlights

Skal Labissière is a professional basketball player for the Sacramento Kings. He is currently the only Haitian-born player in the NBA. Labissière was born in Port-au-Prince on March 18, 1996. During the 2010 earthquake, his family's home collapsed with with Labissière, his mother and his brother inside. All three survived, but were trapped under the debris for three hours. Labissière's legs were trapped, causing them to go numb and he was unable to walk for a few weeks after. A few months after the earthquake, Labissière moved to the United States in Memphis, Tennessee. He later played college basketball at Kentucky, then was drafted by the Phoenix Suns with the 28th pick, then had his draft rights subsequently traded to the Sacramento Kings in the 2016 NBA Draft. Nerlens Noel

A center/power forward for the Oklahoma City Thunder.

Stats Bio Highlights

Nerlens Noel is a professional basketball player for the Oklahoma City Thunder. Noel was born on April 10, 1994 in Malden, Massachusetts to Haitian immigrant parents. He played college basketball for Kentucky, breaking the school record for most blocks in a game, for which he had 12. In 2013, Noel suffered a torn ACL and was forced to sit out the rest of the season. Despite his injury, Noel still declared for the NBA Draft, in which he was selected 6th overall by the New Orleans Pelicans and then subsequently had his draft rights traded to the Philadelphia 76ers. He eventually sat out the 2013-2014 NBA as he recovered from knee surgery. In the 2014-2015 he returned and recieved NBA All-Rookie First Team honors. Check out his highlights!
